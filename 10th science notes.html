Class 10 Science - Complete Notes

Chapter 1: Chemical Reactions and Equations

Key Concepts: Types of reactions (combination, decomposition, displacement, double displacement, redox), balancing chemical equations, effects of oxidation in daily life.

  • Example Reaction: 2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO (combination)
  • Redox Reaction: Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu
  • Rusting: Fe + O₂ + H₂O → Fe₂O₃·xH₂O

Chapter 2: Acids, Bases, and Salts

Key Concepts: Properties, reactions, pH scale, common salts.

  • Acid + Base: HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O
  • pH Importance: Human body ~7.4, acid rain ~5.6
  • Salts: NaCl, Na₂CO₃, NaHCO₃, bleaching powder, washing soda, baking soda.

Chapter 3: Metals and Non-Metals

Key Concepts: Physical and chemical properties, extraction of metals, corrosion, prevention.

  • Reactivity Series: K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Pb > Cu > Ag > Au
  • Corrosion: Rusting of iron, prevention by galvanization, painting.

Chapter 4: Carbon and its Compounds

Key Concepts: Covalent bonding, versatile nature of carbon, hydrocarbons, functional groups, soaps, detergents.

  • Covalent bond: Sharing of electrons (H₂, CH₄)
  • Homologous series: Same functional group, CH₂ difference
  • Soaps vs Detergents: Soap = biodegradable; detergent = works in hard water.

Chapter 5: Periodic Classification of Elements

Key Concepts: Mendeleev’s table, modern periodic table, periodic trends.

  • Trends: Atomic size ↓ across a period, ↑ down a group; metallic character ↑ down a group.
  • Groups: Columns; Periods: Rows.

Chapter 6: Life Processes

Key Concepts: Nutrition, respiration, transportation, excretion in plants and animals.

  • Photosynthesis: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
  • Respiration: Breakdown of glucose to release energy (aerobic/anaerobic)
  • Transportation: Blood, xylem, phloem.

Chapter 7: Control and Coordination

Key Concepts: Nervous system, hormones, coordination in plants and animals.

  • Neurons: Dendrite, axon, synapse.
  • Hormones: Auxin (plants), insulin, thyroxine (animals).

Chapter 8: How Do Organisms Reproduce?

Key Concepts: Modes of reproduction, sexual reproduction in plants/animals, human reproductive system.

  • Asexual: Fission, fragmentation, budding, spore formation.
  • Sexual: Flowers, pollination, fertilization, zygote formation.

Chapter 9: Heredity and Evolution

Key Concepts: Mendel’s laws, sex determination, evolution evidence.

  • Law of segregation: Each trait controlled by pair of alleles.
  • Evolution: Fossils, homologous organs, DNA studies.

Chapter 10: Light – Reflection and Refraction

Key Concepts: Reflection, refraction, spherical mirrors, lenses, mirror formula.

  • Mirror formula: 1/f =1/v +1/u
  • Lenses: Convex, concave; image formation, magnification.

Chapter 11: Human Eye and Colourful World

Key Concepts: Structure of eye, defects, atmospheric refraction, scattering of light.

  • Defects: Myopia, hypermetropia, astigmatism, corrected by lenses.
  • Phenomena: Rainbow, blue sky, red sunset.

Chapter 12: Electricity

Key Concepts: Ohm’s law, resistance, series and parallel circuits, heating effect.

  • Ohm’s law: V = IR
  • Resistors: Rₜₒₜ = R₁ + R₂ (series); 1/Rₜₒₜ =1/R₁ +1/R₂ (parallel)
  • Power: P = VI = I²R

Chapter 13: Magnetic Effects of Electric Current

Key Concepts: Magnetic field, field lines, right-hand thumb rule, electromagnetic induction.

  • Applications: Electric motor, generator, transformer.

Chapter 14: Sources of Energy

Key Concepts: Conventional and non-conventional sources, fossil fuels, renewable energy.

  • Examples: Solar, wind, hydroelectric, geothermal, biomass.
  • Importance: Sustainability, environmental impact.

Chapter 15: Our Environment

Key Concepts: Ecosystem, food chain, waste management, ozone layer.

  • Biodegradable vs Non-biodegradable: Impact on environment.
  • Ozone depletion: CFCs role, prevention strategies.

Chapter 16: Sustainable Management of Natural Resources

Key Concepts: Conservation, reduce-reuse-recycle, case studies (forests, wildlife, dams).

  • Examples: Chipko movement, rainwater harvesting, water conservation.
